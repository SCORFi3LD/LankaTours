Kandy

The secret capital of Sri Lanka! The last Ceylonese king ruled here until 1815. The biggest attraction in Kandy is the Tooth Temple, where a tooth relic of Buddha is kept and carried in the month of Esala (July / August) as part of a Perahera on an elephant through the city.

Just outside of Kandy lies the university town of Peradeniya with the famous Botanical Garden, which was founded in 1371. Wonderful avenues, a rich orchid house and an ayurvedic herb garden are the highlights of this complex, which you should definitely visit.

Also nearby is the elephant orphanage of Pinnawela. Many of the small elephant orphan children have already grown into huge animals and now form a large herd, which may be observed while bathing in the Mahaweli River.

In addition to the famous Temple of the Tooth, there are many smaller temples in the surrounding area, which are architecturally very beautiful. One is the wooden temple of Embekka with beautiful carvings, the other is the Galmaduwa temple in Indian design.
