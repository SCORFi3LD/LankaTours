Polonnaruwa

Since I grew up near Polonnaruwa, this attraction is very important to me. Immerse yourself in the ancient culture of King Parakrama Bahu I. He was one of Sri Lanka's most progressive kings - he built reservoirs all over the island to grow rice year-round.

The old buildings can be visited in a huge park. The former royal palace at that time comprised 500 rooms - one room for each wife; Today you can still see the monstrous outer walls. The ancient royal bath is still well preserved, as well as the different temples for the different faiths of women.

The most impressive in Polonnaruwa is probably the Gal Vihara, the rock temple. Three Buddha statues and a figure of Ananda (Buddha's favorite disciples) are artistically carved out of the rock. A masterpiece of the contemporary sculpture. You too will be amazed by this very special place!

In 1215, the era of Polonnaruwas came to an end, while the southern Indian Maghas destroyed many of the city's buildings and the Rajaratas (land of kings), and the inhabitants fled to the highlands. Only in the 19th century, the ruins of the city were found and began with excavations and restorations.
