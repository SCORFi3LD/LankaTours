Galle - Port city in the south of Sri Lanka

The old town of Galle lies within the fort and invites you as a visitor to walks or to browse through the many boutiques. Flying traders follow you on the newly renovated fort wall to sell old coins and bobbin lace.

The harbor may not be visited, but the fishing port already. Colorful boats, small stalls, interesting faces make great photo opportunities - thank God, the smell is not on the photo.

Galle was a popular port city with the colonial rulers, from here the colonial goods were shipped home. The Portuguese built the fort in the 16th century, and the Dutch and the English continued to build it.

Several museums bear witness to the changing history of Galle and Sri Lanka and are well worth a visit. The curious smorgasbord of the private "Historical Mansion Art Gallery" is especially funny.
