Anuradhapura

This royal capital was founded as early as the 4th century BC. Around an offshoot of the Bodhi tree from India, under which Gauthama Buddha was enlightened, emerged the religious center of the Buddhists and capital of the kings.

For more than 1300 years, Anuradhapura remained the capital of the king and was thus a religious and political center, longer than ancient Athens or ancient Rome. In 993 AD, invading Chola warriors plundered the city and destroyed it.

For you as a visitor of this ancient city, there are many buildings to admire: Huge dagobas, bathing facilities, temples with well-preserved guardian and moonstones and - not least - the sanctuary: the Bodhi tree in the holy district.

Just outside is the small temple of Isurumuniya, whose visit I would like to recommend to you. The special feature here is the small museum with its stone reliefs, u. a. the portrait of two lovers.
