The north of Sri Lanka - the Jaffna peninsula

The north of Sri Lanka - the Jaffna Jaffna peninsula is geographically much closer to India than the capital Colombo. As a result, the Jaffna peninsula and its Tamil inhabitants are Indian, Hindu shaped. The vegetation has adapted to the dry climate, but the soil is still very fertile.

Over the elephant pass or the new bridge at Kerathivu, it is possible for your visitors to travel the North without any problems. There is not much to see of the war-turmoil; the locals are restrained friendly and very accommodating.

The Jaffna Peninsula is full of attractions: the city of Jaffna with its fort, library, and bazaar; the surrounding area with colorful Hindu temples and Agraland; The offshore islands with famous temples and wild horses.

Jaffna is very different than the rest of Sri Lanka and should be touring on a longer trip. I have often been here with guests and know the most beautiful and interesting places.
