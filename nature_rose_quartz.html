Rose Quartz Mountain

On the road from Dambulla to Anuradhapura there is a turnoff to "Jathika Namal Uyana", a forest of ironwood trees and the largest rose quartz mine in Asia. A longer walk - about 30 minutes - leads through a forest of ancient ironwood trees.

Halfway up, you have a wonderful view of the Rose Quartz Mountain. On some sunny days, the quartz should shine so brightly that you absolutely need sunglasses to protect the eyes.

With good shoes you can also hike to the mountain top. Once at the top there is a 360-degree view of the green landscape. Of course, a Buddha statue may not be missing in a Buddhist country.

In Eisenholzwald there is also a small hermitage; The Guardian of the Rose Quartz Mountain Monk Ven Wanawasi Rahula Thero, an excellent rhetorician, lives here.
