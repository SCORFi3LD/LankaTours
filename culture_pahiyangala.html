Pahiyangala - The cave of Fa-Hsien

A very special place between Rathnapura and Kalutera. The Chinese monk Fa-Hsien was said to have lived here for two years during his travels in the 5th century and is named after the cave. It is the largest cave in Sri Lanka (about 85 m deep, 45 m high and 55 m wide), but actually it is "only" a ledge.

The story, however, is much older: the Ceylonese scientist dr. U. Siran Deraniyagala explored the cave in 1968 and 1986. Among other things, he found in the adjacent, smaller cave a human skull bone, which is due to a radiocarbon dating about 38,000 years old. A sensational find - the oldest Homo sapiens of all Asia!

Also, tools made of quartz and hearths were found. Further finds inform about the eating habits of these ancient humans - various game, herbs and ground seeds and nuts. The most beautiful is probably the shrine with a 13 m long, lying Buddha. This one is bronze and very stylish. A very suitable place for a short meditation.

Pahiyangala is a great example of not having to pay a high entry fee to see something very special. I am happy to show you this historical place during your tour.
