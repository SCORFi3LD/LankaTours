The Mannar Peninsula

The Mannar Peninsula is worth the trip, believe me! Even if the guides write that there are no donkeys and a big baobab - it's just not true. We spent four days on the Mannar Peninsula and surrounding area on the first trip, and it did not get boring for a minute.

Already on the journey you can visit the famous shrine Madhu Church. Immediately thereafter, there is a suspension bridge in charming surroundings to visit. Then we continue to the coast, here is the ruin of the "Doric House", the former manor of the first British Governor Frederick North. Nearby, there is the old, dilapidated Arippu Fort.

About a 3 km long bridge, it goes to the Mannar peninsula. Immediately at the entrance of Mannar is the Fort, just behind the Biobab tree, a baobab tree with about 19.5 m circumference. Outside of Mannar there is still the temple Thiruketheeswaram to visit, an important Shiva pilgrimage of the Hindus. Incidentally, you can still watch many birds!
